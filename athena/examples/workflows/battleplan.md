# /battleplan — The Ousen Protocol

> **"I only fight battles I can win."** — Ou Sen, Third Great General of Qin
>
> Ou Sen never commits his army without full intelligence. He sends spies into Gyou, scouts across the mountains, reads every report — and ONLY THEN designs his battle plan. The scouts are expendable. The General's time is not.

## When to Use

- After receiving an assignment brief (post-`/brief` or post-client scoping)
- After deep research has been completed (scouts have returned with intelligence)
- **BEFORE** any writing or execution
- Any deliverable worth $200+ or >2,000 words

## Prerequisites

Before running `/battleplan`, you MUST have:

1. ✅ **Assignment brief** — scoped via `/brief` or client intake
2. ✅ **Deep research output** — intelligence gathered by scouts (Gemini Deep Research, ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.)

The deep research agents are your **scouts**. They go into enemy territory, gather raw intelligence on the topic, and return. This takes ~5 minutes and costs nothing. The General (Athena) then reads the intelligence and designs the battle plan.

```
Flow: Client brief → /brief → Deep Research (scouts) → /battleplan → Red-team audit → Execute
```

---

## The Protocol (7 Phases)

---

### Phase 1 — Intelligence Briefing (Digest the Scouts' Reports)

> Ou Sen's spies infiltrated Gyou and reported back on food supply levels. He read EVERY report before planning.

Read and synthesize ALL deep research outputs:

- [ ] Summarize key findings from each research source
- [ ] Identify consensus positions (what do all sources agree on?)
- [ ] Identify contested positions (where do sources disagree?)
- [ ] Flag citation-rich zones (topics with abundant verified sources)
- [ ] Flag citation-desert zones (topics where sources are thin — hallucination risk HIGH)
- [ ] Extract the 5-10 most powerful arguments/data points discovered

**Output:** Intelligence digest — compressed knowledge map of the topic.

---

### Phase 2 — Terrain Scan (Read the Battlefield)

> Ou Sen rode out to scout Gyou with A Kou before committing a single soldier.

Read EVERY source document the client provided. No skimming.

**Extract:**
- [ ] Assignment title, module code, institution
- [ ] Word count / page limit
- [ ] Submission deadline (calculate working hours remaining)
- [ ] Marking rubric — this IS the enemy's formation. Study it like Ou Sen studies Ri Boku.
- [ ] Specific questions / sub-tasks (number them Q1, Q2, Q3...)
- [ ] Required format (APA, Harvard, specific templates)
- [ ] Case study / prescribed readings (if any)
- [ ] Weightage (% of final grade)

**Output:** Structured brief summary (≤15 lines).

---

### Phase 3 — Examiner's Game Plan (Anticipate the Opponent)

> Ou Sen predicted Ri Boku's every counter-move at Shukai Plains. He knew where Ri Boku would attack before Ri Boku moved.

The examiner is not the enemy — but they ARE the opposing general. Anticipate how they will evaluate this work:

- [ ] **What is the examiner REALLY looking for?** (Read between the rubric lines)
- [ ] **Where do students typically fail on this type of assignment?** (Common traps)
- [ ] **What would make the examiner go "this student gets it"?** (Distinction markers)
- [ ] **What are the trap questions?** (Questions that look simple but require depth)
- [ ] **What's the unmarked minefield?** (Requirements that aren't in the rubric but are expected — e.g., proper academic tone, logical flow, critical analysis vs. description)

**War-game the scenarios:**

| Examiner Move | Our Counter |
|---------------|-------------|
| "This reads like a summary, not analysis" | Embed critical evaluation in EVERY section — compare, contrast, argue |
| "Sources are weak/insufficient" | Pre-verify all citations, maintain source diversity (journals + reports + gov data) |
| "Doesn't address the question" | Map each section back to rubric criteria explicitly |
| "Too descriptive, no original thought" | Include a unique analytical lens or framework application |
| [assignment-specific trap] | [specific counter] |

**Output:** Examiner anticipation matrix.

---

### Phase 4 — KSA Gap Map (Where Are We Still Blind?)

> At Retsubi, Ou Sen realized the supply line was a trap. He identified the gap before it destroyed him.

Even AFTER deep research, honestly assess remaining gaps:

| Q# | Topic | Knowledge (K) | Skill (S) | Ability (A) | Gap Level |
|----|-------|---------------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Q1 | [topic] | ✅/⚠️/❌ | ✅/⚠️/❌ | ✅/⚠️/❌ | 🟢/🟡/🔴 |

**Gap Levels:**
- 🟢 **Green** — Can write now. Deep research confirmed our knowledge.
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Partial. Need 1-2 targeted follow-up searches.
- 🔴 **Red** — Still blind. Deep research didn't cover this. Need secondary research wave.

**Key question:** After the scouts returned, do we have enough intelligence to win? If >30% of gaps are 🔴, we need a SECOND research wave before proceeding.

**Output:** Post-research gap map.

---

### Phase 5 — Battle Formation (Deploy the Troops)

> Ou Sen's formation at Shukai Plains: Gaku Ka on the left, Hi Shin in the center, Gyoku Hou on the right. Every unit had a role BEFORE the battle started.

This is the core of the battle plan — **how you will structure and write the deliverable**:

- [ ] **Section outline** with word count targets (proportional to rubric weight)
- [ ] **Argument architecture** — what is the thesis? What evidence supports it? What are the counter-arguments? How do we synthesize?
- [ ] **Troop deployment** — which research findings go in which section?
- [ ] **Strongest units forward** — front-load your best arguments (first impression bias)
- [ ] **Reserve forces** — which additional points can be deployed if word count allows?
- [ ] **Defensive positions** — where are the weakest sections? How do we fortify them? (acknowledge limitations > fabricate strength)
- [ ] **Visual assets** — what tables, figures, diagrams are needed and WHERE?

**The formation should read like a battle order:**

```
CENTER ARMY (Q1 — 40% of rubric, 40% of word count):
  → Deploy: [Framework X] applied to [Case Study]
  → Supported by: [3 verified sources]
  → Kill shot: [Original analytical insight]

LEFT WING (Q2 — 30% of rubric):
  → Deploy: [Comparative analysis of A vs B]
  → Supported by: [Industry data + academic sources]
  → Risk: Citation desert — prepare defensive position (acknowledge gap)

RIGHT WING (Q3 — 30% of rubric):
  → Deploy: [Recommendations based on findings]
  → Supported by: [Best practice frameworks]
  → Reserve: [Additional examples if word count allows]
```

**Output:** Full battle formation — section-by-section execution plan.

---

### Phase 6 — Win Conditions & Risk Register

> Ou Sen offered Kyou En a position AFTER defeating him. He knew the outcome before the battle started.

**Win Conditions (from rubric):**
- [ ] **Distinction**: What specific markers does the examiner want? (Be precise)
- [ ] **Pass**: What is the minimum viable submission?
- [ ] **Fail**: What hard constraints would cause failure? (plagiarism, wrong format, missed questions)

**Risk Register:**

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Citation hallucination | High (20% base rate) | Critical | Verify ALL refs via Scholar/Crossref |
| Scope creep (over-writing) | Medium | Medium | Hard word cap per section |
| AI detection flag | Medium | High | Humanization pass post-writing |
| Weak section collapses under scrutiny | Medium | High | Defensive positioning (acknowledge limitations) |
| Time crunch | Variable | High | Write lowest-rubric-weight sections LAST |

**Output:** Win condition checklist + risk register.

---

### Phase 7 — Commander's Intent (The One-Line Directive)

> Every Ou Sen battle has a single strategic objective. Even if communications are cut, every subordinate knows the endgame.

Write ONE sentence:

> **Commander's Intent**: "[Deliverable] proves [thesis/argument] by [method], targeting [distinction criteria], supported by [X] verified sources, delivered by [deadline]."

This is the North Star. Every writing decision, every research tangent, every scope question gets tested against this sentence. If it doesn't serve Commander's Intent — kill it.

## Post-Battleplan: The Red-Team Gate

**The battleplan is NOT approved until it survives red-team audit.**

Run `/audit` or manual red-team on the battleplan:

- [ ] Does the formation address EVERY rubric criterion?
- [ ] Are there any 🔴 gaps that would collapse a section?
- [ ] Is the argument architecture logically sound?
- [ ] Are the citation sources real and verified?
- [ ] Does Commander's Intent align with the actual rubric?
- [ ] Would the examiner's anticipated moves be countered by our formation?

**If the battleplan fails audit** → revise before executing. Never commit troops to a flawed plan.

**If the battleplan passes audit** → proceed to execution. Write with confidence — the battle has already been won in your mind.

## Double-Envelope Audit Architecture

> The battle is won in the mind before the first soldier moves. But even Ou Sen verifies after conquest.

```
Red-Team #1 (PRE-BATTLE)              Red-Team #2 (POST-BATTLE)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━           ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Audits the PLAN                       Audits the OUTPUT
"Is our formation sound?"             "Did we execute the plan?"
Catches: wrong thesis, missed         Catches: hallucinated refs,
rubric criteria, citation deserts,    tone drift, AI detection,
structural flaws                      formatting, logical gaps
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━           ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STRATEGIC failures                    EXECUTION failures
(rewrite-level if missed)             (fixable with targeted edits)
```

**Full pipeline:**
```
Brief → Deep Research (scouts) → /battleplan → Red-Team #1 → Execute → Red-Team #2 → Humanize → Ship
```

Execution is near-frictionless because the General already won the battle. The soldiers just march.

## The Ousen Principles (Governing Laws)

1. **Send scouts first, plan second.** — Deep research (5 min) before any planning.
2. **Never fight blind.** — No writing without a battle plan.
3. **Anticipate the opponent.** — Study the rubric like it's Ri Boku's formation.
4. **Every unit has a role.** — Every section satisfies a rubric criterion.
5. **Build natural fortresses.** — Verified citations are your defensive walls.
6. **Only fight battles you can win.** — If 80% of gaps are 🔴 post-research, re-scope or decline.
7. **Win the battle in your mind first.** — The plan IS the victory. Execution is just deployment.
